# Scenario Valuation Guide

Use this guide when valuation is decision-critical. The goal is not precision; it is to expose what the current price already assumes.

## Required Scenario Table

Every formal memo must include at least three scenarios:

## Field Rules

- **Revenue**: State time horizon and currency. Use segment-level drivers when one segment dominates the thesis.
- **Gross Margin**: Explain why it improves, decays, or stays flat.
- **FCF Margin**: Use free cash flow after maintenance capex, working capital, cash taxes, interest, and recurring stock-based compensation pressure where relevant.
- **Dilution / Share Count**: Include share issuance, SBC, convertibles, ATM programs, warrants, or buybacks.
- **Terminal Method**: Use the simplest defensible method: EV/revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E, FCF yield, DCF, sum-of-parts, liquidation value, or asset replacement cost.
- **Implied Return / Gap**: State annualized return, upside/downside to current value, or what future value would be required to justify today's price.
- **Key Assumptions**: List the two or three assumptions that carry the scenario.

## Discipline

- Do not use a scenario if the business mechanism cannot be explained.
- Do not hide the hardest assumption in a terminal multiple.
- Do not treat dilution as harmless for companies with negative free cash flow.
- Do not average scenarios into a false certainty. Use them to reveal dependency on evidence.
- Tie each scenario to monitoring items that would raise or lower confidence.
